Inversion with Conditionals (Had I known...)

Função

  • Dar ênfase, elegância e formalidade ao substituir o "if" em condicionais por uma inversão do verbo auxiliar com o sujeito.
  • Muito usado em textos formais, escritos acadêmicos, discursos e literatura.
Dica: Essa inversão torna a frase mais sofisticada e reduz o uso do "if". Só use em contextos formais!

Estrutura

  • Had + sujeito + particípio passado, ...
  • Should + sujeito + infinitivo sem "to", ...
  • Were + sujeito + complemento, ...
  • Exemplos:
    Had I known the answer, I would have told you.
    Should you need assistance, don't hesitate to call.
    Were it not for you, I wouldn't be here.
TipoEstruturaExemplo
3rd Conditional (passado irreal) Had + sujeito + particípio Had you told me, I would have helped.
1st Conditional (futuro/possibilidade) Should + sujeito + verbo base Should you need help, call me.
2nd Conditional (hipótese presente) Were + sujeito + complemento Were I you, I would apologize.

Exceções e Cuidados

  • Esse tipo de inversão é inadequado na fala informal.
  • Evite misturar a inversão com o uso de "if" na mesma oração.
  • O verbo auxiliar sempre vem antes do sujeito.
  • Com "were", pode ser usada para construir hipóteses: Were I in your position, I’d do the same.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- Had I seen him, I would have spoken to him.
- Had they known, they would have acted differently.
- Were I you, I would apologize.
- Should you require further information, contact us.

Negação:
- Were it not for her, we wouldn't be here.
- Had I not received the message, I would have missed the meeting.
- Should you not agree, please let us know.

Interrogativa/ênfase:
- Should you have any questions, let me know.
- Had you realized the consequences, would you have acted differently?
- Were you to accept the offer, what would you do next?

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar "if" e a inversão juntos: If had I known... (Errado!)
  • ❌ Esquecer o verbo auxiliar antes do sujeito: Had knew I... (Errado!)
  • ❌ Usar em contextos informais ou coloquiais.
Mixed Tenses in Context

Função

  • Permitir que diferentes tempos verbais coexistam em uma narrativa para indicar sequência de eventos, contexto, causas e consequências.
  • Deixar claro quando cada ação ocorreu ou ocorre, criando histórias e explicações mais ricas e precisas.
Dica: Usar tempos verbais mistos é essencial para expressar relações temporais complexas em inglês, especialmente em narrativas, notícias e relatos.

Dicas Gerais

  • Contextualize cada ação: use passado para eventos concluídos, presente para situações atuais e futuro para planos ou previsões.
  • Observe a sequência lógica dos acontecimentos: passado perfeito para indicar uma ação anterior a outra no passado, presente perfeito para conectar passado e presente, etc.
  • Evite misturar tempos verbais sem lógica – cada tempo deve ter um motivo claro.
Situação Tempos Combinados Exemplo
Narrativa de passado Past Continuous + Past Simple + Past Perfect She was studying when the phone rang because she had forgotten to turn it off.
Explicação de causa/efeito Present Perfect + Past Simple I haven't eaten lunch because I was busy all morning.
Descrição geral e eventos específicos Present Simple + Past Simple I usually go to the gym, but yesterday I stayed home.
Relação entre eventos passados Past Simple + Past Perfect He left after he had finished the report.
Planos e rotina Future Simple + Present Simple I will call you when I get home.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- I was walking home when I realized I had lost my keys.
- She hasn't finished her work because she was sick yesterday.
- He will tell you everything when he arrives.
- I have lived here for five years, but I moved from Brazil in 2020.
- She had already left when I got there.

Negação:
- She hasn't finished her work because she was sick yesterday.
- I didn't know you had bought a car.
- He hasn't called me since he started his new job.
- They weren't at home when I arrived because they had gone out.

Interrogativa:
- Did you notice how everyone had left before you arrived?
- Have you ever met someone who didn't like chocolate?
- Will you wait here until I come back?
- Had you ever visited London before last year?

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar o mesmo tempo verbal para todas as ações, perdendo a lógica temporal.
  • ❌ Misturar tempos sem relação causal ou sequencial clara.
  • ❌ Esquecer o uso do passado perfeito para eventos anteriores a outro passado.
  • ❌ Usar o presente perfeito para eventos claramente terminados no passado (use past simple).
Emphatic Structures (What I need is..., The thing is...)

Função

  • Dar ênfase a uma parte específica da frase, destacando informações importantes, sentimentos ou argumentos.
  • Tornar a comunicação mais clara, expressiva e persuasiva.
Dica: Use essas estruturas para deixar claro ao ouvinte/leitor o que é realmente importante na sua mensagem, especialmente em argumentação ou quando precisa de clareza.

Estrutura

  • What/All/The thing (that)... is/was ...
  • What I need is some rest.
  • All I want is to help.
  • The thing is, I forgot the ticket.
  • Estruturas comuns: What I want to say is..., The point is..., All I did was..., The only thing I need is...
EstruturaExemplo
What + frase + is/was + destaque What she wanted was a new opportunity.
All + frase + is/was + destaque All I did was try to help.
The thing/point/fact is... The point is, we have no time left.
Frases interrogativas enfáticas What is it that you want?

Dicas Gerais

  • Essas estruturas são muito úteis para argumentar, introduzir opiniões, reclamações ou justificar decisões.
  • Podem ser usadas tanto em linguagem falada quanto escrita.
  • Também servem para esclarecer mal-entendidos ou corrigir informações.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- All I want is to help.
- What I need is more time.
- The thing is, I lost my wallet.
- What she likes is chocolate.

Negação:
- What I don't like is waiting.
- The thing is, I can't go tonight.
- All he doesn't want is trouble.

Interrogativa/Ênfase:
- What is it that you want?
- What I want to know is why you left.
- The question is, can we finish in time?
- What did you do that for?

Outros exemplos:
- The only thing I need is your support.
- The fact is, we are running out of options.

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar "is" com sujeito no plural sem ajustar: What I need are more friends. (correto, pois "friends" é plural)
  • ❌ Omitir "the thing is" em contexto de explicação: a frase pode ficar abrupta.
  • ❌ Usar sem necessidade, deixando o texto repetitivo ou artificial.

Nominalisation (to write → writing → the writing of...)

Função

  • Transformar verbos ou adjetivos em substantivos, criando frases mais formais, acadêmicas e impessoais.
  • Permite foco na ação ou conceito, não na pessoa que executa.
Dica: Nominalisation é muito usada em textos científicos, relatórios, artigos acadêmicos e linguagem formal.

Estrutura

  • Verbo → Substantivo (geralmente adicionando sufixos como: -tion, -ment, -ance, -ence, -ing, -ity, -ness)
  • Adjetivo → Substantivo (-ity, -ness, -cy)
  • decide → decision
    write → writing → the writing of the letter
    happy → happiness
    efficient → efficiency
  • Estrutura comum: the + [substantivo formado] + of + [objeto]
  • Exemplo: the analysis of the data
Verbo/AdjetivoSubstantivoExemplo em frase
decide decision The decision was unanimous.
inform information The information is confidential.
analyze analysis The analysis of the results was detailed.
move movement The movement of the planets fascinates scientists.
efficient efficiency The efficiency of the process has improved.
happy happiness Happiness is important for well-being.

Dicas Gerais

  • Use nominalisation para soar mais objetivo e evitar excesso de pronomes pessoais.
  • Evite excesso de nominalizações seguidas, pois pode deixar o texto difícil de ler.
  • Prefira em textos formais, relatórios, resumos e artigos acadêmicos.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- The completion of the project was delayed.
- The analysis of the samples took two days.
- The writing of the report is my responsibility.
- The increase in temperature caused concern.

Negação:
- The lack of information prevented a decision.
- There was no confirmation of the results.
- The absence of communication created confusion.

Interrogativa:
- Is the cleaning of the room done every day?
- Has the installation of the equipment been completed?
- Was there an explanation for the delay?

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Exagerar no uso pode deixar o texto pesado: The analysis of the completion of the writing of the project...
  • ❌ Usar nominalisation em textos informais ou conversas do dia a dia (dá um tom artificial).
  • ❌ Esquecer de adaptar o verbo principal: The decide of the case (Errado!) → The decision of the case
  • ❌ Usar nominalisation sem preposição quando necessário: The writing the text (Errado!) → The writing of the text
Discourse Markers (however, nevertheless, in spite of...)

Função

  • Ligar frases, ideias ou parágrafos, organizando o discurso de maneira lógica e fluida.
  • Facilitar a compreensão, indicar contraste, adição, causa, conclusão, sequência etc.
Dica: Discourse markers tornam seu texto mais natural e coeso, sendo essenciais em redações, apresentações e conversas formais.

Exemplos Comuns

Marcador Função Exemplo
however / nevertheless contraste/oposição We tried hard; however, we failed.
in spite of / despite contraste/concessão In spite of the rain, the event continued.
moreover / furthermore / in addition adição She is smart; moreover, she is very kind.
therefore / thus / consequently consequência The product is faulty; therefore, it will be recalled.
on the other hand contraste/alternativa He likes football. On the other hand, his brother prefers tennis.
for example / for instance exemplificação Many animals, for example, lions, are carnivores.
as a result resultado He missed the bus. As a result, he was late.
firstly / secondly / finally sequência Firstly, let’s discuss the main issue.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- The results were disappointing; however, we learned a lot.
- The new policy is effective; moreover, it saves money.
- The weather was cold. Nevertheless, we enjoyed the trip.
- The solution is simple; therefore, everyone can apply it.

Negação:
- In spite of the rain, the event wasn't cancelled.
- Despite his efforts, he didn't succeed.
- The proposal wasn't accepted. On the other hand, we got valuable feedback.

Interrogativa:
- Nevertheless, can we continue with the plan?
- In addition, should we consider other options?
- Therefore, do you agree with the conclusion?

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar discourse markers sem pontuação adequada: However we failed. (Errado!) → However, we failed.
  • ❌ Trocar o sentido dos marcadores: usar "however" para adicionar ideias em vez de contrastar.
  • ❌ Repetir o mesmo marcador muitas vezes; varie conforme a função.
  • ❌ Usar marcadores formais em contextos muito informais ou vice-versa.
Advanced Linkers (not only... but also..., as long as...)

Função

  • Conectar ideias, criar frases compostas e adicionar complexidade ao discurso.
  • Permitir expressar condições, contrastes, adições e sequências de forma sofisticada.
Dica: Advanced linkers deixam seus textos e falas mais naturais, fluentes e avançados, sendo fundamentais em contextos acadêmicos, profissionais e argumentativos.

Estrutura

  • Expressões comuns: not only... but also..., as long as, provided (that), even though, unless, as soon as
  • Not only is she smart, but she is also kind.
  • You can go out as long as you finish your work.
  • Even though it was raining, we went outside.
  • Provided that you pay in advance, we will reserve your seat.
  • Unless you hurry, you’ll miss the train.
  • Call me as soon as you arrive.
Linker Função Exemplo
not only... but also... ênfase/adicionar informação Not only did she win, but she also broke the record.
as long as condição You can go as long as you call me later.
provided (that) condição formal We’ll help, provided that you ask.
even though contraste Even though he was tired, he finished the project.
unless condição negativa You won’t pass unless you study.
as soon as sequência I’ll call you as soon as I arrive.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- Not only did he win, but he also broke the record.
- You can join us as long as you finish your work.
- Provided that you agree, we will proceed.
- Even though it was late, they kept working.
- I’ll let you know as soon as I have news.

Negação:
- Unless you hurry, you won't catch the bus.
- You won’t succeed unless you try.
- He can’t come unless he finishes his homework.

Interrogativa:
- Can we go provided that we finish on time?
- Will you call me as soon as you arrive?
- Would you help me even though you’re busy?

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar "unless" no lugar de "if not" em frases positivas: Unless you study, you will pass (Errado!) → Unless you study, you will NOT pass.
  • ❌ Esquecer a inversão com "not only" no início de frase: Not only she is smart... (Errado!) → Not only is she smart...
  • ❌ Usar "as long as" para tempo (é sempre condição, não duração).
  • ❌ Confundir "even though" (fato real) com "even if" (hipótese).

Função

  • Expressar ideias de forma figurada, natural e avançada, tornando a comunicação mais rica, cultural e próxima de nativos.
  • Transmitir mensagens com mais impacto, humor ou sutileza.
Dica: Idiomatic expressions não devem ser traduzidas literalmente. Sempre busque o significado e o contexto correto!

Dicas Gerais

  • Use idioms para soar mais natural, mas evite em contextos muito formais ou técnicos.
  • Aprenda o significado e veja exemplos antes de usar.
  • Alguns idioms são específicos de regiões/culturas. Quando em dúvida, escolha expressões mais universais.
  • Em escrita acadêmica, prefira linguagem direta e clara.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- The exam was a piece of cake.
- She always goes the extra mile for her clients.
- He hit the books every night last semester.
- We called it a day at 6pm.

Negação:
- I'm not feeling well; I'm under the weather.
- I don't see eye to eye with my colleague.
- He didn’t break the ice at the party.
- She didn't let the cat out of the bag.

Interrogativa:
- Do you often break the ice at meetings?
- Did you spill the beans about the surprise?
- Have you ever missed the boat on an opportunity?
- Why did you throw in the towel so early?

  • ❌ Traduzir ao pé da letra: Estou debaixo do tempo (Errado!) → I'm under the weather (significa "estou indisposto")
  • ❌ Usar idioms fora do contexto ou sem conhecer o verdadeiro significado.
  • ❌ Misturar idioms muito informais em situações profissionais ou acadêmicas.
Subjunctive (If I were you..., It’s essential that he be...)

Função

  • Expressar desejos, hipóteses, conselhos, recomendações, exigências ou situações irreais/contrafactuais.
  • Usado para mostrar que algo não é um fato, mas sim uma possibilidade, desejo ou necessidade.
Dica: O subjuntivo é muito comum em linguagem formal, regras, instruções e para dar conselhos de forma polida.

Estrutura

  • If + sujeito + were + ... (mesmo com "I", usamos "were" no subjuntivo)
    If I were you, ...
  • It's essential/important/necessary that + sujeito + verbo (base)
    It's essential that he be here.
  • Wish + sujeito + were/could/etc.
    I wish I were taller.
  • Suggest/Recommend/Insist/Ask + that + sujeito + verbo (base)
    I suggest that she study more.
  • Negative: that + sujeito + not + verbo (base)
    It's crucial that he not be late.
Estrutura Uso Exemplo
If + sujeito + were Hipótese/Conselho If I were you, I would study more.
It's essential that + sujeito + verbo (base) Recomendação/Exigência It's essential that everyone arrive on time.
I wish + sujeito + were Desejo I wish I were rich.
that + sujeito + not + verbo Proibição It's important that he not miss the meeting.
Suggest/Recommend/Insist/Ask that + sujeito + verbo Sugestão/Recomendação They recommend that she take the test again.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- If I were you, I would call her.
- It's essential that he be here.
- I wish I were taller.
- They suggested that she apply for the job.
- The doctor recommends that he take the medicine.

Negação:
- It's crucial that he not be late.
- I wish it were not raining.
- The teacher insists that students not use their phones.
- It's important that you not forget your passport.

Interrogativa:
- Is it necessary that everyone be present?
- What would you do if you were me?
- Why do they insist that she be the leader?
- Is it important that he not speak during the meeting?

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar "was" ao invés de "were" no subjuntivo: If I was you (Errado!) → If I were you
  • ❌ Esquecer o verbo na base após "that": It's essential that he is here (Errado!) → It's essential that he be here
  • ❌ Traduzir literalmente para o português, pois o subjuntivo em inglês tem regras diferentes.
  • ❌ Usar o subjuntivo em contextos informais onde não é necessário.
Formal vs. Informal Structures

Função

  • Diferenciar o uso da língua conforme o contexto (e-mails profissionais, reuniões, cartas vs. conversas, mensagens, redes sociais).
  • Adequar o registro linguístico ao público e à situação para transmitir respeito, profissionalismo ou proximidade.
Dica: Estruturas formais são preferidas em ambientes profissionais, acadêmicos ou desconhecidos. Estruturas informais são apropriadas entre amigos, familiares ou em comunicações rápidas.

Exemplos

FormalInformal
I would appreciate it if you could...Can you...?
Furthermore, ...Also, ...
It is required that...You have to...
I regret to inform you...Sorry, but...
Could you please clarify...Can you explain...?
We would like to invite you to...Come join us...
I am writing to request...I'm asking for...
Should you need any assistance...If you need help...
I am unable to attend...I can't make it...
We apologize for any inconvenience.Sorry for the trouble.

Dicas Gerais

  • Observe seu público, objetivo e contexto antes de escolher o registro.
  • Estruturas formais usam frases mais longas, voz passiva, vocabulário polido, e evitam contrações e gírias.
  • Estruturas informais usam frases curtas, contrações, gírias, linguagem direta e descontraída.
  • Evite informalidades em situações profissionais ou com desconhecidos.
  • Prefira o formal em e-mails de trabalho, cartas oficiais, apresentações, e o informal com amigos, família e conversas cotidianas.

Exemplos

Formal:
- I would be grateful if you could send the documents.
- I'm afraid I can't attend the meeting.
- Could you let me know your availability?
- It is with great pleasure that I invite you to the event.
- We appreciate your cooperation.

Informal:
- Can you send me the papers?
- Sorry, I can't go to the meeting.
- When are you free?
- Come to the party!
- Thanks for your help.

Negação (Formal):
- I regret to inform you that I am unable to participate.

Negação (Informal):
- Can't make it, sorry!

Interrogativa (Formal):
- Would you mind confirming your attendance?
- May I ask you a question?

Interrogativa (Informal):
- Are you coming?
- Got a minute?

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar gírias ou contrações em e-mails formais: I'll get back to you asap (Errado em formal!)
  • ❌ Ser excessivamente formal com amigos: Would you be so kind as to call me? (Estranho em informal!)
  • ❌ Misturar registros no mesmo texto ou conversa sem motivo claro.
Modality in Past (should have done, ought to have...)

Função

  • Expressar arrependimento, crítica, obrigação, conselho, possibilidade ou probabilidade sobre ações ou situações passadas.
  • Permitir que o falante comente sobre o que era esperado, possível ou aconselhável no passado.
Dica: Estruturas com "should have", "could have", "might have", "ought to have", etc. são essenciais para analisar decisões ou eventos já concluídos.

Estrutura

  • Sujeito + should/could/might/may/ought to/must/can't + have + past participle
  • You should have called me. He might have missed the train. They could have helped. She ought to have apologized. He must have forgotten. You can't have seen her yesterday.
  • Negativa: should/could/might + not + have + past participle
    They shouldn't have lied. He might not have noticed.
  • Interrogativa: Should/Could/Might + sujeito + have + past participle?
    Should I have sent the email?
Modal Significado Exemplo
should have obrigação/conselho não realizado You should have studied more.
could have capacidade ou possibilidade não concretizada She could have won the race.
might have possibilidade/incerteza He might have missed the train.
may have possibilidade They may have left already.
ought to have dever moral/ético You ought to have told the truth.
must have certeza/conclusão lógica He must have forgotten the meeting.
can't have impossibilidade You can't have seen her yesterday.

Dicas Gerais

  • Utilize essas estruturas para analisar e comentar sobre eventos já finalizados, demonstrando domínio do tempo verbal e do contexto.
  • Não confunda com modais no presente (should do, could go, etc.).
  • Observe o sentido específico de cada modal (crítica, possibilidade, obrigação, etc.).

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- She could have won the race.
- He must have left early.
- You ought to have apologized.
- They might have missed the flight.
- You should have listened to me.

Negação:
- They shouldn't have lied.
- He can't have finished already.
- She might not have received the message.
- You shouldn't have spent so much money.

Interrogativa:
- Should I have sent the email?
- Could he have forgotten our meeting?
- Might they have misunderstood the instructions?
- Ought you to have told her the truth?

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar o infinitivo em vez do particípio passado: You should have go (Errado!) → You should have gone
  • ❌ Esquecer o "have" após o modal: You should called (Errado!) → You should have called
  • ❌ Confundir "should have" (passado) com "should" (presente).
  • ❌ Usar "mustn't have" para impossibilidade (preferir "can't have").
Advanced Reported Speech (with time shifts)

Função

  • Relatar falas, pensamentos ou perguntas de terceiros, fazendo as adaptações necessárias em tempos verbais, pronomes, advérbios de tempo e lugar.
  • Permite transmitir informações de forma indireta, mantendo a lógica temporal correta.
Dica: Reported speech é fundamental para narração, relatórios, notícias e provas avançadas de inglês.

Mudanças Comuns

  • Tempos verbais:
    • Presente simples → Passado simples
      "I am tired" → He said he was tired.
    • Presente contínuo → Passado contínuo
      "I am working" → She said she was working.
    • Passado simples → Past perfect
      "I saw her" → He said he had seen her.
    • Futuro (will) → would
      "I will call you" → He said he would call me.
    • Can/can't → could/couldn't
      "I can help" → She said she could help.
  • Pronomes e determinantes: adaptados conforme o contexto.
    "my" → "his/her"
  • Advérbios de tempo e lugar:
    • "now" → "then"
    • "today" → "that day"
    • "tomorrow" → "the next/following day"
    • "yesterday" → "the previous day"
    • "here" → "there"
  • Passado perfeito permanece igual (She said she had left.)

Dicas Gerais

  • Se o verbo introdutor estiver no presente (ex: He says), não é necessário mudar o tempo verbal.
  • Use "that" opcionalmente após o verbo de fala: He said (that) he was tired.
  • Em perguntas, troca-se a ordem para afirmativa: "Where is he?" → She asked where he was.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- He said (that) he would call me the next day.
- She told me (that) she was working late.
- He mentioned (that) he had finished the report.
- They explained (that) they could not attend the meeting.
- John remarked (that) the project was going well.

Negação:
- She said she hadn't seen him.
- He said he couldn't come to the party.
- They told me they wouldn't arrive on time.
- He reported that he hadn't received the message.

Interrogativa:
- Did they say when they would arrive?
- She asked if I had finished my homework.
- He wanted to know why I was late.
- She asked where the documents were.
- He inquired whether the meeting had started.

Outros exemplos:
- "I'm leaving now," she said. → She said (that) she was leaving then.
- "Can you help me?" → He asked if I could help him.
- "We saw her yesterday." → They said they had seen her the previous day.

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Esquecer de ajustar pronomes e advérbios de tempo/lugar (ex: "here" → "there").
  • ❌ Não mudar o tempo verbal quando o verbo introdutor está no passado.
  • ❌ Manter estrutura de pergunta direta em perguntas reportadas (usar ordem afirmativa).
  • ❌ Esquecer "that" após o verbo de fala (opcional, mas deixa o texto mais claro).
Hedging and Softening Language (apparently, it seems...)

Função

  • Tornar afirmações menos diretas, mais educadas, menos absolutas e evitar soar ríspido ou categórico.
  • Expressar incerteza, opinião pessoal ou evitar confrontos em comunicações profissionais e sociais.
Dica: Hedging é essencial para manter a diplomacia, polidez e profissionalismo, especialmente ao dar feedbacks, sugerir ideias ou relatar informações não confirmadas.

Exemplos comuns

Expressão Uso Exemplo
apparently indica algo que parece ser verdade, mas não é certo Apparently, the meeting has been postponed.
it seems / it appears indica impressão, não certeza It seems that she’s not available.
probably / possibly indica probabilidade She will probably arrive late.
I suppose / I guess opinião moderada I suppose we could try again.
kind of / sort of / a bit reduz intensidade I'm a bit tired today.
would / could / might modalizadores para sugestões ou dúvidas Would it be possible to reschedule?
to some extent limitar afirmação That’s true to some extent.
as far as I know limitar ao conhecimento pessoal As far as I know, the project is on track.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- Apparently, the meeting has been postponed.
- It seems that the problem has been solved.
- She is probably on her way.
- I suppose we could start without him.
- I'm a bit concerned about the results.
- To some extent, I agree with you.
- As far as I know, the report is finished.

Negação:
- It doesn't seem that they agree.
- I don't think it's the best option.
- She probably won't be able to join us.
- I wouldn't say that's completely true.
- It's not exactly what I expected.

Interrogativa:
- Would it be possible to reschedule?
- Could we consider another approach?
- Might you have the data by tomorrow?
- Do you think it might rain later?
- Is there a chance we could postpone the meeting?

Dicas Gerais

  • Use hedging para suavizar opiniões, sugestões, críticas ou quando não tem certeza absoluta.
  • Evite usar de forma excessiva para não parecer indeciso ou inseguro.
  • Combine com linguagem positiva e construtiva em ambientes profissionais.

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar frases muito diretas em contextos delicados: You are wrong. (Errado!) → I'm not sure that's correct.
  • ❌ Utilizar hedging em excesso, tornando o discurso vago ou pouco assertivo.
  • ❌ Não adaptar o grau de suavização ao contexto (formal ou informal).
Reduced Relative Clauses (The man talking to her...)

Função

  • Simplificar frases eliminando palavras como "who", "which", "that", seguidos de "is/are/was/were", tornando o texto mais conciso e natural.
  • Deixar o inglês mais avançado e fluente, especialmente na escrita formal ou acadêmica.
Dica: Use reduced relative clauses para evitar repetições e tornar descrições mais elegantes e objetivas.

Estrutura

  • Cláusula relativa completa: The man who is talking to her →
    Reduced: The man talking to her
  • Cláusula relativa completa: The car that was stolen →
    Reduced: The car stolen
  • Regra geral:
    • Para ações em andamento (ativa): verbo + ing The boy running in the park
    • Para ações já concluídas (passiva): particípio passado The cake made yesterday
Cláusula Completa Cláusula Reduzida Tipo
The man who is talking to her The man talking to her Ativa (ação em progresso)
The car which was stolen The car stolen Passiva (ação já realizada)
The students who are studying The students studying Ativa
The book that was written by her The book written by her Passiva
The people who live here The people living here Ativa
The building that was constructed in 2010 The building constructed in 2010 Passiva

Dicas Gerais

  • Use para tornar textos mais naturais, claros e avançados.
  • Prefira em redações, apresentações, descrições e narrativas formais.
  • Reduzir relative clauses ajuda a evitar frases muito longas ou repetitivas.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- The man talking to her is my teacher.
- The students studying in the library are from Brazil.
- The cake made by my mother was delicious.
- The car parked outside belongs to John.

Negação:
- The books written by her are famous.
- The ideas presented yesterday were innovative.
- The people invited to the event are experts.

Interrogativa:
- Who is the person sitting next to you?
- Which car stolen last week was found?
- Do you know the girl talking to Peter?

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar reduced clause para frases sem sujeito definido: Is talking to her the man my teacher? (Errado!)
  • ❌ Aplicar a redução em frases sem verbo auxiliar apropriado.
  • ❌ Confundir uso de -ing (ação em andamento) com particípio (ação já concluída/passiva).
  • ❌ Deixar frases ambíguas ou sem clareza ao omitir informações essenciais.
Complex Passive Structures (He is said to have...)

Função

  • Expressar opiniões, crenças, informações indiretas, rumores e expectativas de maneira formal e impessoal.
  • Muito usado em reportagens, textos acadêmicos/científicos, relatórios e contextos onde o agente da ação não é o foco.
Dica: O Complex Passive é ideal para dar ênfase na informação, e não em quem a transmitiu.

Estrutura

  • Fórmula: Sujeito + is/are/was/were + said/believed/thought/known/expected/reported/considered + to + verbo base
    He is said to be rich.
  • Para ações no passado: Sujeito + is/was said/believed... + to have + particípio passado
    She is thought to have left the country.
  • Negativa: Sujeito + is/was not said/believed... + to (have + particípio)
    She is not believed to have stolen the money.
  • Interrogativa: Is/Was + sujeito + said/believed... + to...?
    Is it thought to be possible?
Verbo Passivo Significado Exemplo
is/are said to opiniões/rumores gerais The city is said to be very expensive.
is/are believed to crenças gerais He is believed to be honest.
is/are thought to opinião coletiva The solution is thought to be effective.
is/are expected to expectativa The president is expected to arrive soon.
is/are known to fato reconhecido She is known to speak five languages.
is/are reported to informação de notícia Several people are reported to have been injured.
is/are considered to opinião geral This work is considered to be a masterpiece.

Dicas Gerais

  • Comum em textos jornalísticos, acadêmicos e relatos formais.
  • Evita identificar o agente da ação (quem disse, achou, etc.), tornando o texto impessoal e objetivo.
  • Use "to have + particípio" para se referir a ações passadas.
  • Variação para plural: They are believed to have left.

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- The president is expected to arrive soon.
- He is said to be very generous.
- The city is believed to be safe.
- The scientists are thought to have discovered a new planet.
- The results are considered to be reliable.

Negação:
- She is not believed to have stolen the money.
- The building is not expected to collapse.
- The rumors are not thought to be true.

Interrogativa:
- Is it thought to be possible?
- Are they believed to have left the country?
- Is the result said to be accurate?

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar o verbo principal no passado direto: He is said to was rich (Errado!) → He is said to be rich ou He is said to have been rich
  • ❌ Omitir o "to" na estrutura.
  • ❌ Confundir com a voz passiva simples ("is done", "was built"), que não transmite opinião ou rumor.
  • ❌ Usar "to have" com ações claramente presentes.
Advanced Conditionals (Unless, provided that...)

Função

  • Expressar condições usando estruturas alternativas ao "if", tornando o discurso mais rico, variado e preciso.
  • Permitir maior flexibilidade para situações hipotéticas, restritivas, permissivas ou preventivas.
Dica: Advanced conditionals demonstram domínio de estruturas sofisticadas em inglês, ideais para redações, argumentos e negociações.

Exemplos comuns

Condicional Uso Exemplo
unless condição negativa Unless you call, I won’t know what happened.
provided (that) condição restritiva You can go out provided that you finish your work.
as long as condição permissiva You can borrow my car as long as you drive carefully.
even if contraste com condição I’ll go even if it rains.
in case precaução/prevenção Take an umbrella in case it rains.
supposing (that) hipótese Supposing (that) he doesn’t come, what will you do?

Exemplos

Afirmação:
- You can go out provided that you finish your work.
- I’ll join you as long as I’m free.
- Take your phone in case you get lost.
- I’ll go even if no one else does.

Negação:
- Unless you call, I won’t know what happened.
- You won’t succeed unless you try.
- We can’t start unless everyone is here.

Interrogativa:
- What will you do in case it rains?
- Will you come even if you’re tired?
- Supposing he doesn’t answer, what should we do?
- Can I leave as long as I finish the report?

Dicas Gerais

  • Evite usar "unless" para condições positivas; ele sempre nega a condição.
  • "Provided (that)" e "as long as" são usados para permissões condicionais, mas "provided" é mais formal.
  • "In case" expressa prevenção, não é sinônimo de "if".
  • Use "even if" para mostrar que o resultado acontece apesar da condição.
  • Em redações, varie o uso de condicionais para enriquecer o texto.

Erros comuns

  • ❌ Usar "unless" no lugar de "if" para condições positivas: Unless you study, you will pass. (Errado!) → Unless you study, you will NOT pass.
  • ❌ Usar "in case" com o sentido de "if": Call me in case you arrive late. (Errado! Use "if".)
  • ❌ Confundir "provided that" (condição restritiva) com "even if" (contraste).
  • ❌ Repetir o mesmo condicional muitas vezes em um texto.
